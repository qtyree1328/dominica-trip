See Sperm Whales Up Close

Dominica is the only place on Earth with a year-round resident population of ~200-300 sperm whales. Boat tours get you close enough to watch fluking dives, surface socializing, and calves alongside their mothers.

Waterfalls & Rainforest

Hike through pristine rainforest to thundering waterfalls — Trafalgar Falls, Middleham Falls, Titou Gorge. More hiking trails per square mile than anywhere in the Caribbean.

Champagne Reef

Snorkel over volcanic vents that release streams of warm bubbles through the sea floor. It literally looks like swimming in champagne. One of the Caribbean's most unique underwater sites.

Eco-Luxury Retreat

Stay at a world-class eco resort — treehouse villas perched on cliffs above the Caribbean Sea, private plunge pools, farm-to-table dining, and total immersion in nature.

The Whale Experience

Everything you need to know about seeing sperm whales in Dominica

Why Dominica?

Dominica hosts the only year-round resident population of sperm whales in the world. Approximately 200-300 sperm whales — primarily females and their offspring — live in the deep waters off the west coast.

The island's west coast drops off steeply to depths of 1,000+ meters just a few miles offshore — perfect habitat for sperm whales, who forage on deep-sea squid at 300-1,000m depth.

April is excellent for sightings — calmer seas, whales are active with calves, and strong chance of observing spinner dolphins, spotted dolphins, and pilot whales alongside the sperm whales.

Boat-Based Whale Watching

Dive Dominica

Half-day afternoon tours, $69/person, 3-3.5 hrs. Departs 2 PM from Roseau. Hydrophone tracking, rum punch included. Runs 1-3x per month. divedominica.com

Waitukubuli Tours

Similar boat tours bookable on Viator/TripAdvisor. Highly reviewed. Private charters also available (~$688 for up to 6 people).

What to Expect on a Boat Tour

Sightings

Watch sperm whales surface, breathe, and perform their iconic fluke-up dives. Mothers with calves, logging pods, and dramatic tail slaps. Sighting rate is ~80%+.

Dolphins

Huge pods of spinner, spotted, Fraser, and bottlenose dolphins are almost guaranteed. They'll ride the bow wake and leap alongside the boat.

Hydrophone Listening

Crews use directional hydrophones to locate whales by their echolocation clicks before they surface. Haunting and unforgettable.

Photography

Bring a camera with zoom lens. Surface behaviors make for incredible shots with Dominica's volcanic mountains as backdrop. Binoculars recommended.

Practical Info

Everything else you need to know

Currency & Money

  • EC$ (Eastern Caribbean Dollar) — 1 USD ~ 2.70 EC$
  • US dollars widely accepted
  • ATMs in Roseau and Portsmouth
  • Credit cards accepted at resorts & restaurants
  • Carry cash for taxis, small shops, tips

Getting Around

  • Taxis: Arranged through resort. Airport transfer ~$60-80 USD
  • Rental car: Available but roads are mountainous & narrow. Driving is on the LEFT
  • Most resorts arrange all transport for excursions
  • Roseau to Portsmouth: ~1.5 hrs drive

Packing List

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (mandatory!)
  • Waterproof camera / GoPro
  • Hiking shoes with grip (trails are muddy)
  • Quick-dry clothes
  • Rain jacket (it's a rainforest!)
  • Snorkel mask (or use operator's)
  • Insect repellent
  • Waterproof phone case

Weather in April

  • Avg high: 86F (30C)
  • Avg low: 73F (23C)
  • Dry season — less rain than summer
  • Sea conditions: generally calm on west coast
  • Water temp: 80F (27C)
  • Sunrise ~5:50 AM / Sunset ~6:20 PM

Top Restaurants

  • Pearl's Cuisine — Authentic Creole (Roseau)
  • Cocorico Cafe — French-Caribbean (Roseau)
  • The Ruins Rock Cafe — Seafood in stone ruins (Roseau)
  • Pagua Bay House — Creative Caribbean
  • Bwa Denn — Secret Bay's farm-to-table
  • Zing Zing — Secret Bay beach bar
  • Fort Young Hotel — Waterfront dining

Practical Tips

  • Entry: US passport — no visa needed
  • Language: English (official) + Creole
  • WiFi: Available at resorts, spotty elsewhere
  • Power: 230V, UK-style plugs (Type D/G) — bring adapter
  • Tipping: 10-15% at restaurants
  • Safety: Very safe island. Low crime.
