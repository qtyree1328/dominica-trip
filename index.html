🐋 See Sperm Whales Up Close

Dominica is the only place on Earth with a year-round resident population of ~200–300 sperm whales. Boat tours get you close enough to watch fluking dives, surface socializing, and calves alongside their mothers — with dolphins riding your bow wake.

🌿 Waterfalls & Rainforest

Hike through pristine rainforest to thundering waterfalls — Trafalgar Falls, Middleham Falls, Titou Gorge. Dominica has more hiking trails per square mile than anywhere in the Caribbean.

🫧 Champagne Reef

Snorkel over volcanic vents that release streams of warm bubbles through the sea floor. It literally looks like swimming in champagne. One of the Caribbean's most unique underwater sites.

🌅 Eco-Luxury Retreat

Stay at a world-class eco resort — treehouse villas perched on cliffs above the Caribbean Sea, private plunge pools, farm-to-table dining, and total immersion in nature.

🐋 The Whale Experience

Everything you need to know about swimming with sperm whales in Dominica

Why Dominica?

Dominica hosts the only year-round resident population of sperm whales in the world. Approximately 200–300 sperm whales — primarily females and their offspring — live in the deep waters off the west coast. These aren't migratory visitors; they're family pods that have lived here for generations.

The island's west coast drops off steeply to depths of 1,000+ meters just a few miles offshore — perfect habitat for sperm whales, who forage on deep-sea squid at 300–1,000m depth.

April is excellent for sightings — calmer seas, whales are active with calves, and you have a strong chance of observing multiple species including spinner dolphins, spotted dolphins, and pilot whales alongside the sperm whales.

Two Very Different Experiences

🚤 Boat-Based Whale Watching (Available for your trip)

Dive Dominica: Half-day afternoon tours, $69/person, 3–3.5 hrs. Departs 2 PM from Roseau. Hydrophone tracking, rum punch included. Runs 1–3× per month. divedominica.com

Waitukubuli Tours: Similar boat tours bookable on Viator/TripAdvisor. Highly reviewed. Private charters also available (~$688 for up to 6 people).

🏊 In-Water Swim Encounters (Requires separate trip planning)

This requires a week-long expedition package. The Dominica government issues 10-day swim permits ($4,000–$6,000 USD) only to licensed operators, who run fixed-schedule, multi-day trips with max 6 guests. There are no single-day swim options.

Operators & pricing:

  • One With Whales — ~$5,500–$6,500/person, 7 nights, Sat–Sat. onewithwhales.com
  • Sperm Whale Swims — $6,650+/person, 7 nights, includes Fort Young Hotel. spermwhaleswims.com
  • Just the Wild — ~$3,500–$4,500/person, 6 nights. justthewild.com
  • whale-expeditions.com — From €2,400/person, 8 days. Has an Apr 25–May 2 session (starts day you leave!)
  • Immersiv Expeditions — 5 swim days + 1 land day. immersivexpeditions.com

💡 If this is a bucket-list priority, consider planning a separate dedicated trip around an operator's schedule. Most run Nov–June. Book months ahead — they sell out.

🎯 What to Expect on a Boat Tour

👀 Sightings

Watch sperm whales surface, breathe, and perform their iconic fluke-up dives from the boat. Mothers with calves, logging pods, and dramatic tail slaps are all possible. Sighting rate is ~80%+.

🐬 Dolphins

Even on days when sperm whales are elusive, huge pods of spinner, spotted, Fraser, and bottlenose dolphins are almost guaranteed. They'll ride the bow wake and leap alongside the boat.

🔊 Hydrophone Listening

Crews use directional hydrophones to locate whales by their echolocation clicks before they surface. You may get to listen to the clicks through a speaker — haunting and unforgettable.

📸 Photography

Bring a camera with zoom lens. Surface behaviors (breathing, fluking, spy-hopping) make for incredible shots with Dominica's volcanic mountains as backdrop. Binoculars recommended too.

📋 Practical Info

Everything else you need to know

💰 Currency & Money

  • EC$ (Eastern Caribbean Dollar) — 1 USD ≈ 2.70 EC$
  • US dollars widely accepted
  • ATMs in Roseau and Portsmouth
  • Credit cards accepted at resorts & restaurants
  • Carry cash for taxis, small shops, tips

🚗 Getting Around

  • Taxis: Arranged through resort. Airport transfer ~$60–80 USD
  • Rental car: Available but roads are mountainous & narrow. Driving is on the LEFT
  • Most resorts arrange all transport for excursions
  • Roseau to Portsmouth: ~1.5 hrs drive

🎒 Packing List

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (mandatory!)
  • Waterproof camera / GoPro
  • Hiking shoes with grip (trails are muddy)
  • Rashguard for whale encounters
  • Quick-dry clothes
  • Rain jacket (it's a rainforest!)
  • Snorkel mask (or use operator's)
  • Insect repellent
  • Waterproof phone case

🌤️ Weather in April

  • Avg high: 86°F (30°C)
  • Avg low: 73°F (23°C)
  • Dry season — less rain than summer
  • Sea conditions: generally calm on west coast
  • Water temp: 80°F (27°C) 🤤
  • Sunrise ~5:50 AM / Sunset ~6:20 PM
