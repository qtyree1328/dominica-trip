🥾 Hikes & Trails

▼

Boiling Lake Trail

The ultimate Dominica hike. 6-mile round trip to the second-largest boiling lake in the world, a flooded fumarole shrouded in steam. Passes through the Valley of Desolation with sulfur vents, hot springs, and alien landscapes.

Difficulty: Strenuous Duration: 6–7 hrs Guide: Required Cost: ~$50–80/pp (guide)
Book via Waitukubuli Tours

Middleham Falls

Moderate hike through montane rainforest to one of the Caribbean's tallest waterfalls — a 200-foot cascade into a deep swimming pool. Giant ferns, parrots overhead.

Difficulty: Moderate Duration: 3 hrs round trip Guide: Recommended Cost: Free (guide ~$30)

Waitukubuli National Trail (Segments)

The Caribbean's longest hiking trail (115 miles, 14 segments). You can do individual segments as day hikes. Segment 5 (Pont Casse to Castle Bruce) and Segment 10 (Morne Anglais) are popular. Spectacular ridge-line views.

Difficulty: Varies Duration: 4–8 hrs per segment Cost: Site pass $12/week
Official Trail Site

Morne Trois Pitons

UNESCO World Heritage site. Hike to the summit for panoramic views of the island, or explore the surrounding trails through cloud forest. The park contains Boiling Lake, Middleham Falls, and Freshwater/Boeri Lakes.

Difficulty: Moderate–Strenuous Duration: 4–6 hrs Guide: Recommended

Syndicate Nature Trail (Parrot Watching)

Easy loop trail through the Syndicate rainforest — the best place to spot Dominica's two endemic parrots: the Imperial parrot (Sisserou) and the Red-necked parrot (Jaco). Early morning is best.

Difficulty: Easy Duration: 1–2 hrs Cost: Free

Freshwater Lake & Boeri Lake

Two volcanic crater lakes high in the mountains. Freshwater Lake (easy, paved path) is Dominica's largest. Boeri Lake requires a moderate hike from Freshwater Lake through elfin woodland. Mystical, often clouded in mist.

Difficulty: Easy (Freshwater) / Moderate (Boeri) Duration: 1–3 hrs Cost: $5 entry

Cabrits National Park / Fort Shirley

Restored 18th-century British fort on a volcanic headland. Easy trails through dry forest with panoramic views of Prince Rupert Bay. Great bird watching.

Difficulty: Easy Duration: 1–2 hrs Cost: $5 entry

💧 Waterfalls & Gorges

▼

Trafalgar Falls

Dominica's most iconic — twin waterfalls (Father & Mother) plunging side-by-side. Short 15-min walk. Climb the boulders to reach hot springs at the base for a natural jacuzzi.

Location: Trafalgar, near Roseau Walk: 15 min Entry: $5 Swimming: Yes + hot springs

Middleham Falls

200-foot waterfall in pristine rainforest with a deep swimming pool at the base. One of the tallest in the Caribbean.

Location: Laudat / Morne Trois Pitons NP Walk: 1.5 hrs each way Entry: Free Swimming: Yes

Titou Gorge

Swim through a narrow volcanic gorge with sheer canyon walls. Surreal blue-green water leads to a hidden waterfall inside a cave. Pirates of the Caribbean filming location.

Location: Laudat Entry: ~$5 Difficulty: Moderate (swimming in current) Must-do: Yes

Emerald Pool

Gentle 20-minute walk through rainforest to a grotto with a picture-perfect waterfall into a crystal-clear swimming pool. Easy and beautiful.

Location: Morne Trois Pitons NP (east side) Walk: 20 min Entry: $5 Swimming: Yes

Victoria Falls

Remote, powerful waterfall requiring a river-crossing hike. Less visited and more adventurous than Trafalgar or Middleham. The pool at the base has natural hot spots from volcanic activity.

Location: White River area, Delices Walk: 2–3 hrs round trip Guide: Required (river crossings) Swimming: Yes

Sari Sari Falls

Towering waterfall on the southeast coast accessed via a steep trail and river crossings. One of Dominica's most dramatic — worth the effort. Close to La Plaine.

Location: La Plaine, southeast Walk: 45 min each way Guide: Recommended Swimming: Yes

Spanny Falls

Smaller, less-visited waterfall near Belles. Easy short hike, great for a quick swim. A locals' favorite.

Location: Belles Walk: 30 min Entry: Free

🤿 Snorkel & Scuba

▼

Champagne Reef

Volcanic vents release thousands of warm bubbles through the seafloor. Swim through effervescence over healthy coral. Surreal and easy — Dominica's most unique snorkel site.

Location: Soufriere, south of Roseau Type: Snorkel / Dive Difficulty: Easy Cost: ~$30–50 guided / free if you bring gear

Scotts Head Drop-Off

Where the Caribbean meets the Atlantic. Sheer wall dive with 80+ ft visibility. Snorkeling right off the beach is excellent too. Pristine coral, sponges, and reef fish.

Location: Scotts Head, southern tip Type: Snorkel / Dive Difficulty: Easy (snorkel) / Intermediate (wall dive)

Soufriere Pinnacles

Dramatic underwater volcanic spires covered in sponges. Home to seahorses, frogfish, and macro life. One of the Caribbean's best dive sites.

Location: Soufriere Bay Type: Scuba only Difficulty: Intermediate

Toucari Bay

Calm, shallow bay on the northwest coast. Great for easy snorkeling — coral gardens, reef fish, occasional sea turtles. Near Portsmouth / Secret Bay.

Location: Northwest coast Type: Snorkel Difficulty: Easy

Coral Garden

Shallow reef just south of Salisbury. Pristine hard and soft corals, very accessible. Good for both snorkeling and intro dives.

Location: Salisbury, west coast Type: Snorkel / Dive Difficulty: Easy

Dive Operators

Anchorage Dive Center — Anchorage Hotel, Castle Comfort. Full PADI center. 1-tank $60, 2-tank $95.

Nature Island Dive — Soufriere. Small operator, great for south coast sites. 1-tank $55, 2-tank $90.

🐋 Whale Watching

▼

Dive Dominica — Whale Watch Tour

Half-day boat tour from Roseau. Hydrophone tracking to locate sperm whales. Watch 45-foot giants surface and fluke-dive against the mountain backdrop. Dolphins ride the bow wake. Rum punch on return.

Price: $69/person Duration: 3–3.5 hrs Departs: 2:00 PM (check-in 1:30) Frequency: 1–3x/month
Book at divedominica.com

Waitukubuli Tours — Whale & Dolphin Safari

Similar boat-based tours, highly reviewed on TripAdvisor and Viator. Multiple departure options. Also offers combo nature tours.

Price: ~$65–80/person Duration: 3–4 hrs
waitukubulitours.com

Private Whale Charter

Dive Dominica offers private whale safari boats for up to 6 people. Dedicated hydrophone, your own schedule. Great for small groups.

Price: $688 total (up to 6 ppl) Duration: 3–4 hrs Value for 2: $344/pp
Inquire at Dive Dominica

What You'll See

Dominica has ~200–300 resident sperm whales year-round (the only such population on Earth). April is excellent — calmer seas, active calves. Sighting rate ~80%+. Also spinner, spotted, Fraser, and bottlenose dolphins, plus occasional pilot whales. Crews use hydrophones to find whales by their echolocation clicks.

🏖️ Beaches

▼

Batibou Beach

Often called Dominica's most beautiful beach. White sand (rare on the island), turquoise water, framed by coconut palms and jungle-covered mountains. Remote and usually empty.

Location: Northeast coast, near Calibishie Sand: White Facilities: None (bring supplies)

Champagne Beach

Volcanic pebble beach right next to Champagne Reef. Warm water, easy snorkel entry. The beach itself has warm volcanic spots in the shallows.

Location: Soufriere, south of Roseau Sand: Dark volcanic pebbles Facilities: Small bar/restaurant nearby

Mero Beach

Dominica's most popular and accessible beach. Dark volcanic sand, calm water, food vendors, locals hanging out. Close to Roseau.

Location: West coast, near Roseau Sand: Black volcanic Facilities: Food vendors, restrooms, parking

Purple Turtle Beach

Near Portsmouth on the northwest coast. Long stretch of dark sand, calm Caribbean water. Close to Secret Bay and Cabrits. Good for a relaxed swim.

Location: Portsmouth, northwest Sand: Dark Facilities: Nearby restaurants

Hampstead Beach

Two secluded beaches on the northeast coast. One has a river flowing into the sea. Jungle backdrop, rarely visited. Raw and beautiful.

Location: Northeast coast, near Calibishie Sand: Mixed Facilities: None

Pointe Baptiste

Red-tinted sand beach with dramatic rocky outcrops. A rare red sand beach in the Caribbean. Near Calibishie on the northeast coast.

Location: Calibishie, northeast Sand: Red Facilities: None

🛍️ Shopping & Culture

▼

Old Market, Roseau

Historic stone market building in the center of Roseau. Now houses craft vendors, spice stalls, and souvenir shops. Good for local art, straw bags, and bay oil products.

Location: Central Roseau Hours: Mon–Sat 8 AM–4 PM

Roseau Market (New Market)

Lively farmers' market. Fresh tropical fruit, root vegetables, spices, hot sauces. Busiest on Saturday mornings. The real deal — this is where locals shop.

Location: Roseau riverfront Best day: Saturday morning

Dominica Essential Oils & Spices

Dominica produces some of the world's best bay oil, cinnamon, vanilla, and nutmeg. Look for locally made essential oils, spice bundles, and bay rum (traditional aftershave/fragrance). Available at market stalls and small shops across the island.

Macoucherie Rum Distillery

Small, family-run rum distillery in the hills. One of the oldest in the Caribbean. Tour the operation and sample raw cane rum. Very rustic and authentic.

Location: Macoucherie, west coast Cost: Free tour / small fee

Kalinago Territory (Carib Territory)

Home of the indigenous Kalinago people. Visit for traditional basket weaving, cassava bread making, and learn about Kalinago history and culture. The Kalinago Barana Aute is a cultural village with demonstrations.

Location: East coast Cost: ~$10 entry for cultural village

Botanical Gardens, Roseau

40-acre gardens in the center of Roseau. Home to a school bus crushed by a baobab tree during Hurricane David (1979) — now a famous landmark. Good for a stroll.

Location: Central Roseau Cost: Free

Practical Info

Everything else you need to know

Currency & Money

  • EC$ — 1 USD ≈ 2.70 EC$
  • US dollars widely accepted
  • ATMs in Roseau and Portsmouth
  • Credit cards at resorts & restaurants
  • Carry cash for taxis, small shops, tips

Getting Around

  • Taxis: Resort-arranged. Airport transfer ~$60–80
  • Rental car: Roads are mountainous & narrow. Driving on the LEFT
  • Most resorts arrange excursion transport
  • Roseau to Portsmouth: ~1.5 hrs

Packing List

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (mandatory)
  • Waterproof camera / GoPro
  • Hiking shoes with grip (muddy trails)
  • Quick-dry clothes
  • Rain jacket (it's a rainforest)
  • Insect repellent
  • Waterproof phone case
  • Power adapter (230V, UK-style Type D/G)

Weather in April

  • High: 86°F (30°C) / Low: 73°F (23°C)
  • Dry season — less rain than summer
  • Calm seas on west coast
  • Water temp: 80°F (27°C)
  • Sunrise ~5:50 AM / Sunset ~6:20 PM

Entry & Logistics

  • Entry: US passport — no visa needed
  • Language: English (official) + Creole
  • WiFi: At resorts, spotty elsewhere
  • Tipping: 10–15% at restaurants
  • Safety: Very safe island, low crime

Book Ahead

  • Whale watching tours run 1–3x/month — book ASAP
  • Dive Dominica: divedominica.com
  • Waitukubuli Tours: waitukubulitours.com
  • Boiling Lake guide — arrange through hotel
